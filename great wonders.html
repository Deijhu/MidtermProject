Sunflower Farm

The sunflower farm in Piddig, Ilocos Norte is a privately-owned flower farm that features a picturesque landscape of sunflower maze, bougainvillea, blue ternetea, roses and assorted in-season vegetables. The farm is located about 6 km from the town proper and also houses a coffee roasting facility and food factory school where various skills training courses are offered. The two-hectare spectacle mirrors the best practices in sustainable agriculture of the town. The farm also boasts of an “Instagrammable” lagoon with center island gazebo, a hobbit house or reading nook, petting zoo of farm animals and a fish pond where visitors can grill their catch

Basi Revolt Monument

On September 16, 1807, the Basi Revolt, also known as the Ambaristo Revolt, broke out in Piddig, Ilocos Norte, arising from the Spanish rulers' banning of the private manufacture of the local wine called "basi". As Ilocanos were forced to buy from government stores, wine-loving Ilocanos, led by Salarogo Ambaristo and Pedro Mateo, rose in revolt, which later spread to nearby towns. Spanish troops eventually quelled the revolt on September 28, 1807. "Basi" is the local beverage of the Ilocos, where it has been consumed even before the Spanish conquest. Notably, in San Ildefonso town in Ilocos Sur, a non-working holiday is declared every September 16 in commemoration of the Basi Revolt. In nearby Naguilian, La Union, a Basi Festival is held annually.
